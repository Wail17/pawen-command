Section 5: Refining the Output

First draft won't be perfect. Use these follow-up prompts to improve.



Problem

Follow-up prompt

Hook is weak or generic

"Rewrite the hook using the [format name] format more clearly. Make it more specific and visceral."

Low point doesn't hit hard enough

"Expand the low point. Make it more specific. Include resignation, not just frustration. Use sensory details."

Transformation feels vague

"Make the transformation more progressive — show what changed week 1, week 2, week 3. Add unexpected discoveries."

Dialogue sounds fake

"Rewrite the dialogue to sound like real speech. Short. Fragments. How people actually talk."

Close feels salesy

"Rewrite the close to feel more natural. Connect back to the hook. Add risk reversal and permission."

Doesn't sound like examples

"Match the rhythm and tone of my examples more closely. Shorter sentences. More contrast. More specificity."

Too long

"Cut this to [X] words. Keep the hook, low point, transformation, and close. Tighten the mechanism section."

Too short

"Expand this to [X] words. Add more detail to the low point and transformation. Add a secondary character with dialogue."
