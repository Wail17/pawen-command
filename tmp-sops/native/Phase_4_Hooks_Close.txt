Phase 4: Hooks and close: 



Section 1: What a Hook Does

The hook has just one job: Create enough curiosity or tension that they have to keep reading.



A hook works when it does one of these:

Mechanism

What it triggers

Fear / Danger

"Am I at risk? I need to know."

Broken System

"Finally someone gets why nothing has worked."

Credibility

"This person knows something I don't."

Relatability

"That's exactly what I'm going through."

Curiosity

"Wait — what? I need to understand."

Validation

"Someone else noticed. It's not in my head."

Urgency

"I might be running out of time."

Every hook format in this module triggers one of these.

Section 3: Matching Hook to Awareness Level

Not every hook works for every audience. Match the mechanism to where they are.



Awareness Level

Where they are

Best mechanisms

Best formats

Problem Aware

They know something's wrong. Don't know why or what to do.

Fear / Danger, Relatability, Broken System

Hidden Danger, Raw Embarrassing Truth, Age + Problem Emerging, Caregiver Watching, Failed System Journey

Solution Aware

They've tried things. Been disappointed. Looking for something different.

Broken System, Authority, Curiosity

Conventional Solution Fails, I Refused the Standard Path, Authority Breaking Point, Patient Story + Question, I Beat the Expert

Product Aware

They know your product. Seen your ads. Need final push.

Validation, Urgency, Transformation

Someone Noticed, Transformation Lead, Seasonal Urgency, Warning Signs



The pattern:

Problem Aware — Lead with their pain or fear. Show you understand.

Solution Aware — Lead with why other things failed. Show you're different.

Product Aware — Lead with proof or urgency. Give them reason to act now.









Section 4: What a Close Does

The close transitions from story to sale.

Done wrong — it breaks the native feel. Reader enjoyed the story, senses the pitch coming, so they leave.

Done right — clicking feels like the natural next step. Not a sale. A decision they already made in their mind. 



A close works when it does one of these:

Mechanism

What it triggers

Fear + Path Out

"I don't want to end up like that — here's my way out"

Permission

"It's okay to want this. I deserve this."

Risk Reversal

"I have nothing to lose by trying."

Urgency / Scarcity

"If I don't act now, I might miss it."

Social Proof

"Others like me have done this. It's safe."





The key:

The close should connect back to the hook and theme. The same fear or desire that pulled them in should push them to act.

Hook opened a loop → Close resolves it

Hook raised a fear → Close offers the path out

Hook promised transformation → Close shows how to get it

Fear + Path Out

Don't Wait Warning

Fear from hook returns — but now with escape

Don't wait until [bad outcome] + do something now

"So don't wait until you have a stroke or a sudden heart scare. We have to take care of our own health because the system isn't going to do it for us."



Time Running Out

The damage is compounding — every day matters

Every day you wait + [consequence compounds] + don't wait until it's too late

"Every day you wait is another day of joint inflammation. Every day the damage grows. Every day closer to starting medication you might never be able to stop."



I Can't Go Back

Narrator's regret — reader still has chance

I can't go back + but you can start today + don't wait like I did

"I can't go back to March. But you can start today. Please don't wait until you understand this the way I did."

Permission

You Deserve This

Gives reader permission to want it

You deserve [outcome] + it's okay to want this

"Your age is experience. Your skin should show strength, not neglect."



Not About Vanity

Reframes the want as legitimate

This isn't about [shallow thing] + it's about [deeper thing]

"This isn't about anti-aging or looking younger. It's about having skin that functions. That feels good. That doesn't make you want to hide."

Risk Reversal

Guarantee + Nothing to Lose

Removes risk of trying

[X-day] guarantee + if you don't [outcome], get refund + nothing to lose

"And the best part is they offer a 30-day money-back guarantee. Try it, see if you feel a difference, and if you don't, they'll refund every penny."



Try and See

Low commitment framing

You won't know unless you try + [easy next step]

"Your timeline might be different. But you won't know unless you try."

Urgency / Scarcity

Stock Warning

Scarcity creates action

They sell out + last time took [X months] to restock + grab it now

"I grabbed two extra. Because I know they sell out. Last time it took them almost three months to restock."



Seasonal Window

Time-based urgency

[Outcome] takes [X weeks] + start now, not when you panic

"Real skin transformation takes time. 12 weeks to rebuild collagen. Start now. Not in May when you panic."

Social Proof

Numbers + Trust

Others have done this — it's safe

"450,000 women stopped accelerating their collagen loss. Shop now."



Relatable Reluctance

Narrator was hesitant too — normalizes action

I was skeptical + I finally tried + glad I did

"I had given up trying, and assumed nothing would give me relief. If anyone is interested, I'll leave the link where I found them."

Authority Reinforcement

Credentials + Recommendation

Trust the expert one more time

After [X years] + [credential] + this is what I recommend

"After 14 years and 15,000+ cats, I've finally found something that's both safe and actually works. Your cat deserves that."



P.S. Credibility Stack

Adds final proof after main close

P.S. [Additional credibility or proof point]

"P.S. I don't get paid by them to recommend this. I recommend it because after testing every major brand, they're the only ones who passed independent third-party testing with 99.7% purity."













Section 6: Common Mistakes To Avoid: 



Hook mistakes:

Mistake

Why it fails

Starting with the product

Reader doesn't care about your product yet. They care about their problem.

Too vague

"I struggled for years" — nothing to see, no specificity, no reason to keep reading.

Too dramatic for the reader

If their situation isn't that severe, they disconnect. "That's not me."

Too mild

No tension, no fear, no curiosity. Nothing pulling them forward.

Clickbait with no payoff

Creates curiosity you can't deliver on. Reader feels cheated. Trust breaks.

Generic hook for everyone

Trying to speak to everyone speaks to no one. Best hooks are specific.

Hook doesn't match awareness level

Problem aware reader gets product aware hook. They're not ready. They scroll.



Close mistakes:

Mistake

Why it fails

Switching tone suddenly

Entire ad feels like a story. Close suddenly feels like a sales page. Native feel breaks.

No connection to hook/theme

Hook raised a fear. Close doesn't resolve it. Reader feels incomplete.

Too pushy

"BUY NOW! LIMITED TIME! DON'T MISS OUT!" — triggers ad radar. They leave.

Too weak

No urgency, no risk reversal, no reason to act now. Reader thinks "maybe later." Later never comes.

Forgetting risk reversal

Reader wants to try but fears wasting money. No guarantee = no click.

Only one reason to act

Stack reasons: fear + permission + risk reversal + scarcity. One isn't enough.







Before You Move On

You should have:

☐ Hook format chosen — matches your audience's emotional state and awareness level

☐ Hook written — specific, not vague. Creates curiosity, fear, or recognition.

☐ Close format chosen — connects back to hook and theme

☐ Close written — transitions from story to sale without breaking native feel

☐ Risk reversal included — guarantee, nothing to lose, easy to try

☐ Multiple reasons to act — not just one. Stack fear + permission + urgency + proof.



Closing Note

The hook gets them in. The close gets them to act.

Everything in between — the story, the elements, the psychology — only matters if you nail these two moments.
